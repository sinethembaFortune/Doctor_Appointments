Primary Care

Primary healthcare is the concept of holistic healthcare for all. Instead of disparities between those who have medical aid and those who don’t, primary healthcare offers the same level of care for everyone.

Specialty Care

refers to health services provided by doctors who specialize in a specific area of medicine. Some examples of specialty care include cardiology (heart), dermatology (skin), endocrinology (hormones), gastroenterology (digestive system), neurology (nervous system), and many others. These doctors typically have more in-depth knowledge and training in their specialty area, so they can provide more specialized care.

Preventive Care

refers to health services aimed at preventing diseases or detecting them at an early stage, before symptoms appear. This can include routine screenings, vaccinations, and lifestyle counseling. The goal of preventive care is to help people stay healthy and avoid serious health problems down the road.

Diagnostic Services

refers to tests and procedures used to find out what's causing a health problem. This can include blood tests, imaging tests (like X-rays or MRIs), and biopsies (taking a sample of tissue for testing). Diagnostic services help doctors determine the right treatment for a health problem.

Chronic Disease Management

refers to care for people with ongoing health conditions that last for a long time, like diabetes or heart disease. The goal of chronic disease management is to help people with these conditions live as healthy and productive a life as possible. This can involve a variety of treatments, from medication to lifestyle changes.

Mental Health Services

refers to care for people with mental health conditions, like depression, anxiety, or schizophrenia. This can include therapy, medication, and other treatments. Mental health services are important for maintaining overall health and well-being.

  • How often should I come in for a check-up?

    In general, most adults should have an annual check-up, or a physical exam, with their primary care provider. This allows the provider to check on overall health, including blood pressure, weight, and any chronic conditions. Depending on age and health history, some adults may be advised to have check-ups more or less frequently

  • Which health screenings are recommended for me currently?

    For adults, general recommendations include regular checkups, blood pressure checks, cholesterol screening, diabetes screening, colon cancer screening, and mammograms. Again, the specific recommendations will depend on a person's individual health history, so it's best to talk to a healthcare provider for personalized recommendations.

  • Why is vaccination important?

    Vaccination is a safe and effective way to prevent disease and save lives. When we get vaccinated, we do not just protecting ourselves, but also those around us. Some people, like those who are seriously ill,are advised not to get certain vaccines – so they depend on the rest of us to get vaccinated and help reduce the spread of disease.
